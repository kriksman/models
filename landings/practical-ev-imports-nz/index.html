Model comparison

What varies between practical EV options from Japan

Not all used Japanese EVs are the same. Here's a practical breakdown of the key differences worth considering before you set a brief.

Factor What you need to know
Year range Many budget-friendly practical EVs are from the early to mid-2010s with 24–30 kWh packs, while newer ex-lease cars bring 40–64 kWh batteries. Later years often bring better range and safety tech, and usually meet NZ entry standards if documentation is clean.
Battery / powertrain Smaller city EVs tend to have 24–30 kWh batteries aimed at urban use, while newer or larger models step up to 40–64 kWh for longer trips. Higher-capacity packs usually cost more but reduce how often you need to charge.
Trim differences Higher trims may add features like heated seats, better infotainment, extra airbags or smarter cruise systems. Base trims can be better value if you mainly care about battery health and range.
NZ compliance Later-model EVs typically meet NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Earlier cars may need closer checks on safety features and any aftermarket parts noted on the auction sheet.
Auction availability Common practical EVs appear regularly at Japanese auctions, which helps with choice and pricing. Less common models may take longer to source and can attract stronger bidding when they do appear.
