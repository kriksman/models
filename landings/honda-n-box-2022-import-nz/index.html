Feature deep dive

What makes the Honda N-Box 2022 practical?

For NZ buyers, the 2022 Honda N-Box stands out as a compact commercial option that’s easy to live with day to day. The 3395mm length and 1475mm width make it simple to park in tight city spots, while the 1790mm height and boxy shape give you more usable space than most small hatchbacks. The 660cc engine with around 43 kW of power is built for low-speed, stop–start work rather than motorway sprints, which suits urban deliveries and service calls. Honda Sensing adds modern driver-assist features that many older small vans don’t offer, which is useful if staff will be sharing the vehicle.

If you’re mainly doing short trips with modest loads, this is a solid match.

FAQ

Questions before importing

Is a 2022 Honda N-Box big enough for my business?

If you mainly carry small parcels, tools or light equipment, the tall cabin and folding rear seats usually give enough space. For heavy or bulky loads, a larger van may be more suitable and we can suggest alternatives.

How much does it cost to import a 2022 Honda N-Box to NZ?

Recent examples have typically landed between about $20,000 and $40,000 depending on grade, mileage, 4WD and condition. The total includes the auction price, our fee, shipping, insurance, NZ entry work and on-road costs like registration and plates.

Will a 2022 Honda N-Box meet NZ compliance rules?

A 2022 Japanese-market N-Box typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted, but final approval depends on physical inspection at entry certification. We target cars that look suitable on paper and highlight any risks before bidding.

How long does it take from bidding to having the car on my driveway?

Once we win an auction, shipping to NZ is usually around 2–4 weeks, then you need time for clearance, entry certification and registration. In most cases you’re looking at roughly 6–10 weeks end to end, depending on shipping schedules and workshop queues.

What auction grade should I look for on a Honda N-Box?

Many buyers aim for grade 4 or better with a tidy interior grade, but a well-described lower grade can still be fine if the issues are cosmetic. We go through the sheet, photos and notes with you so you understand what you’re getting.
