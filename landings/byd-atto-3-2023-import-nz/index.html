Battery health

Battery health on a 2023 BYD Atto 3

On a late-model EV like the 2023 BYD Atto 3, outright battery failure is uncommon, but how the car has been used still matters. We look for signs of sensible charging habits and normal daily use so you’re not buying someone else’s experiment. Where data is available, we compare indicated range and usage to what you’d expect from a 60kWh pack.

Look for consistent mileage that suggests regular commuting rather than long storage

Home charging

Charging a BYD Atto 3 in NZ

Most owners will charge a 2023 BYD Atto 3 at home overnight using a wall-mounted charger or upgraded socket. With a 60kWh battery, an overnight charge is usually enough to go from low to full, and many people just top up a bit each night.

Public charging

On the road, the Atto 3 supports DC rapid charging up to 220kW on compatible chargers, so you can add a useful chunk of range in a short stop. Slower AC public chargers are handy for top-ups while you’re at work or shopping.

  • • Fast charging is handy, but relying on it every day can be harder on any EV battery over time.
  • • Public charger availability and pricing vary by region, so it’s worth checking the networks you’ll use most.
  • • If you live in an apartment or shared parking, plan where and how you’ll charge before committing.

Auction price
The hammer price you pay for the car in Japan, plus local auction fees and our service fee.

FAQ

Questions before importing

Is a 60kWh BYD Atto 3 enough battery for daily NZ driving?

For most people, yes. The estimated 420 km official range comfortably covers typical commuting, school runs and errands, with plenty left over. Even allowing for real-world conditions, many owners only need to charge a few times a week.

How much does it cost to import a 2023 BYD Atto 3 from Japan?

Most 2023 Atto 3s we see land between about $45,000 and $60,000, depending on auction price, mileage and trim. On top of the hammer price you’ll need to allow for our fee, shipping and insurance, NZ entry certification, port charges and on-road costs.

Will a Japanese BYD Atto 3 meet NZ compliance rules?

A 2023 BYD Atto 3 typically meets NZ entry standards in most cases if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted, but final sign-off only happens after physical inspection here. We avoid cars with obvious red flags on the auction sheet to improve the odds of a smooth process.

How long does it take from winning an auction to driving the car in NZ?

Once the car is bought, shipping to NZ is usually around 2–4 weeks depending on sailings, then you need to allow time for port processing, entry certification and registration. In total, many buyers see 6–10 weeks from winning the auction to being on the road.

How do auction grades relate to the condition of a BYD Atto 3?

Auction grades give a shorthand view of body and interior condition, but the notes and photos matter just as much. We translate the sheet, highlight any issues and only recommend cars where the grade, mileage and condition line up sensibly for the price.
