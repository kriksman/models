What a Japan-import IS 300h F Sport gets you

This IS 300h is powered by a 2.5-litre petrol engine paired with Lexus Hybrid Drive. The electric motor can move the car quietly at low speeds, the petrol engine joins in automatically when stronger acceleration is needed, and the battery recharges while driving through regenerative braking. There is no need to plug it in. Fuel use is typically around 6 litres per 100 kilometres, which is excellent for a luxury sports sedan. The F SPORT package then adds the visual and chassis attitude: unique bumpers, F SPORT mesh grille, exclusive alloy wheels, sports suspension tuning, F SPORT steering wheel, supportive sports seats, LFA-inspired instrument cluster, sportier trim and F SPORT badging.

What sets it apart03

What sets it apart

These are the technologies and factory features worth understanding because they change how the car drives, feels and costs to run.

Lexus Hybrid Drive

Lexus Hybrid Drive uses a petrol engine, electric motor, high-voltage battery and electronic power-split transmission. The car can move away quietly on electric power, then blend in the 2.5-litre petrol engine when the driver asks for more acceleration. The battery charges itself while driving and through regenerative braking, so there is no plug-in routine.

Lexus Safety System+

Lexus Safety System+ is the active driver-assistance package. Depending on exact specification, it normally brings Adaptive Radar Cruise Control to help maintain distance on the motorway, Lane Departure Assist to warn if the car drifts from its lane, Automatic High Beam for night driving and Road Sign Assist for speed-sign recognition.

F SPORT package

F SPORT is Lexus' factory sports package. On the IS it is highly desirable because it adds the aggressive spindle grille, F SPORT bumper treatment, exclusive wheels, sports suspension tuning, sports seats with extra side support, F SPORT steering wheel and a moving instrument cluster inspired by the Lexus LFA supercar.

Comfort, media and visibility equipment

The auction list includes reversing camera, Bluetooth, factory sunroof, power front seats, LED headlights, ventilated and heated front seats, Blind Spot Monitoring and heated steering wheel. Blu-ray playback is a nostalgic bonus, letting the factory multimedia system play Blu-ray video or music when parked.

Powertrain06

Lexus Hybrid Drive and rear-wheel-drive balance

The IS 300h uses a 2.5-litre petrol engine, electric motor and Lexus Hybrid Drive e-CVT, with power sent to the rear wheels. At low speed it can glide away on electric power. Under acceleration, the petrol engine and electric motor work together. When slowing down, regenerative braking turns some of the car's kinetic energy back into electricity for the hybrid battery. Rear-wheel drive changes the feel as well: the front tyres are not trying to both steer and pull the car, so the IS feels more settled, more neutral and more natural through corners than many front-wheel-drive hybrids.
