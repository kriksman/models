The reason enthusiasts insist on manual

The MX-5 is not about chasing the biggest power figure. It is about the connection: short lever travel, clean mechanical engagement, a light clutch and a rear-wheel-drive chassis that responds instantly. Mazda’s six-speed SKYACTIV-MT is one of the best modern manual gearboxes, and it is the heart of the car’s resale appeal.

FAQ16

Questions before importing

Which Mazda MX-5 manual should I import from Japan?
For enthusiast buyers, we usually start with Mazda Roadster RS Manual 2020+ and Roadster RF RS Manual 2020+. We also check S Special Package and NR-A listings when condition, mileage and price make sense.
Is the Mazda Roadster the same as the Mazda MX-5?
In Japan the car is commonly listed as Mazda Roadster, while New Zealand buyers know it as Mazda MX-5. We confirm the exact trim, engine, roof type and transmission from the Japanese auction sheet before bidding.
Why choose the manual MX-5 instead of automatic?
The six-speed manual is a major part of the MX-5 appeal: short lever travel, precise shifts, light clutch feel and a more direct connection with the rear-wheel-drive chassis.
What mileage and auction grade should I target?
A sensible brief is Grade 4 or higher, under 60,000 km where budget allows, clean auction comments, standard drivetrain and no signs of heavy track use or poor modifications.
What other Mazda manuals are worth importing?
Mazda3 Fastback Manual 2020+ is the strongest practical alternative. Mazda3 Hatchback Manual, Mazda2 Sport Manual and Roadster RF Manual can also suit buyers who want manual engagement with different levels of practicality.
