Battery health

Battery health on Ioniq 5 and EV6 imports

Battery condition is central to choosing between Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6 from Japan. While these are relatively new models, usage patterns and fast-charging habits still matter. We focus on listings with clear service records or battery checks where available, and avoid cars with obvious warning signs.

Look for documented battery checks or service records in the auction notes.
Consider odometer reading alongside age to understand how heavily the car has been used.
Ask about fast-charging history if it’s mentioned, as heavy DC use can affect long-term capacity.
Be open to slightly higher mileage cars if the price reflects it and the battery history looks sensible.

Home charging

Charging Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6 in NZ

Most Ioniq 5 and EV6 owners in NZ rely on overnight home charging. A dedicated wall unit makes the most sense, but even a standard socket can work if your daily driving is modest and you plan ahead.

Public charging

Both models are well suited to NZ’s public charging network, with fast-charging capability on most trims. That makes intercity trips manageable, especially if you plan stops around known DC sites.

  • • Check the charging port type on the specific Japan-market car to confirm it matches what you expect in NZ.
  • • If you do frequent long trips, focus on trims with faster DC charging capability and larger batteries.
  • • Some imported cables may not be ideal for NZ sockets, so you may want to budget for a local charging solution.

FAQ

Questions before importing

Is the Hyundai Ioniq 5 or Kia EV6 better for NZ driving?

They share a lot under the skin, so it mostly comes down to shape and feel. The Ioniq 5 is a bit more upright and family-focused, while the EV6 feels sportier. We can talk through how you drive and suggest which is likely to suit you more.

How much does it cost to import an Ioniq 5 or EV6 from Japan?

Total cost depends on battery size, trim, year and auction price on the day. On top of the auction price you’ll have shipping, insurance, NZ entry certification, on-road costs and our fee. We give you a clear estimate before you commit to bidding.

Do these models typically meet NZ entry standards?

As late-model EVs, Hyundai Ioniq 5 and Kia EV6 typically meet NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final entry certification can only be confirmed after inspection in NZ.

How long will it take to get my imported Ioniq 5 or EV6?

Once we win an auction, shipping to NZ is usually around 2–4 weeks, plus time for clearance and entry certification. Finding the right car at auction can be quick or take a few weeks depending on your budget and spec requirements.

What auction grade should I look for on an Ioniq 5 or EV6?

Most buyers aim for mid to higher grades that balance condition and price. We explain what each grade means, highlight any repair notes and help you decide whether a particular car is worth bidding on.
