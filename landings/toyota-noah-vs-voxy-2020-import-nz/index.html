Why Kiwis choose the Noah / Toyota Voxy

The 2020 Toyota Noah and Voxy are popular with NZ families and community groups who need seven or eight seats without going to a full‑size van. Sliding doors make school runs and tight car parks easier, and the tall cabin suits child seats and taller adults. Petrol use is typically reasonable for the size, especially on open‑road trips.

Questions before importing

Is the 2020 Toyota Noah or Voxy better for a NZ family?
For most families, they’re very similar in space and practicality. The choice usually comes down to which look you prefer and which trim level you can find within your budget. We can show you real auction examples of both so you can compare features.
How much does it typically cost to import a 2020 Noah or Voxy?
Total cost depends on auction price, mileage, grade and trim level. On top of the hammer price you add shipping, insurance, NZ port charges, entry certification, compliance work, registration and our fee. We provide an estimated landed range before you decide to bid.
Do 2020 Noah and Voxy models usually meet NZ entry standards?
usually, 2020 examples with clean documentation and no major modifications typically meet NZ entry standards. Final approval is always decided at NZ entry certification after physical inspection. We flag any potential issues we see on the auction sheet before bidding.
How long does it take from winning an auction to driving in NZ?
From a successful bid, shipping to NZ is typically 2–4 weeks depending on sailings. Entry certification, compliance and registration usually add another couple of weeks, depending on workshop schedules. We keep you updated at each stage.
What auction grade should I look for on a 2020 Noah or Voxy?
Many buyers aim for mid‑to‑high grades where condition and price balance out, but it depends on your budget and how fussy you are about cosmetics. We explain what each grade and panel note means and share the original auction sheet so you can make an informed call.
