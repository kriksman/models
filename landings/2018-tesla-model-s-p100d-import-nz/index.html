Home charging

Charging a 2018 Tesla Model S P100D in NZ

Most NZ owners charge a Model S P100D at home overnight using a dedicated wall unit or suitable socket, topping up the battery while the car sits idle. That routine usually covers daily commuting and school runs without needing public chargers.

Public charging

For longer trips, you can use NZ’s growing network of public AC and DC fast chargers. The P100D’s fast-charging capability means you can add useful range during a coffee or lunch stop, as long as you plan your route around available chargers.

  • • Check what charging hardware and cables are supplied with the car from Japan.
  • • Allow for the cost of installing a home wall charger if you don’t already have one.
  • • Rural routes may have fewer fast chargers, so plan ahead for long-distance travel.
  • • Charging speeds can vary depending on battery temperature and state of charge.

FAQ

Questions before importing

What sort of range can I expect from a 2018 Tesla Model S P100D in NZ?

Real-world range depends on driving style, terrain and battery health, but many owners see less than the original brochure figures. With a healthy battery and mixed NZ driving, you can plan for comfortable intercity trips with a charging stop along the way.

How much does it cost to import a 2018 Tesla Model S P100D from Japan?

Most buyers land these between about $30,000 and $75,000, depending on mileage, condition and auction price. On top of the hammer price, you need to allow for shipping, insurance, NZ entry certification, on-road costs and our service fee.

Will a 2018 Tesla Model S P100D meet NZ entry standards?

Based on auction documentation, this model usually meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final entry certification is done in NZ, so we can't confirm the outcome until the car has been inspected here.

How long does it take from winning an auction to driving the car in NZ?

From a successful bid, shipping to NZ typically takes 2–4 weeks depending on sailings, then you need time for clearance, entry certification and registration. In most cases, buyers are driving their cars within about 6–10 weeks of auction day.

How do auction grades relate to the condition of a used Tesla?

Auction grades are a shorthand for overall condition, but the notes and photos matter just as much. We translate the sheet, point out repairs, wear and any mention of battery or charging issues, then give you our view so you can decide whether the car suits your expectations.
