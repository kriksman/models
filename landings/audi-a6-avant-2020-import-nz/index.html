Questions before importing

Is a 2020 Audi A6 Avant a good daily driver in NZ?
For most buyers, yes — it’s a comfortable wagon with plenty of space and modern safety tech. As long as you keep on top of servicing and use a workshop that knows European cars, it usually works well as a daily. We can help you target lower-kilometre examples with solid service history.
How much does it cost to import a 2020 Audi A6 Avant from Japan?
Total cost depends on the auction price, exchange rate, shipping, insurance, port charges, entry certification, compliance work, our fee and on-road costs. Once we’ve seen a real auction example that matches your brief, we can give you a typical landed-cost range before you decide to bid.
Will a 2020 Audi A6 Avant usually meet NZ compliance standards?
usually, a 2020 A6 Avant from Japan typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no major modifications or structural repairs are noted. Final approval always depends on physical inspection at entry certification in NZ.
How long does it take to get my Audi A6 Avant after we win an auction?
From auction win to the car being ready in NZ, you’re typically looking at around 6–10 weeks. That includes export prep in Japan, shipping, port processing, entry certification, compliance work and on-road steps.
What auction grade should I aim for on a 2020 A6 Avant?
Most buyers look for grade 4 or better with a tidy interior grade, but sometimes a lower grade with honest cosmetic issues can offer value. We explain the grade, translate key comments and highlight anything that might need mechanical or cosmetic attention once it’s in NZ.
