Battery health — what to look for

For a 2026 Leaf, the high-voltage battery is central to your range and long-term usability. Newer Leafs benefit from improved battery management, but individual history still matters. We review available battery data, usage patterns and auction notes to help you choose a car whose battery suits your plans.

  • Check reported battery capacity or health indicators on the auction sheet where available.
  • Review odometer and use type to gauge how many charge cycles the battery may have seen.
  • Avoid cars with warning lights or comments suggesting unresolved battery or charging issues.
  • Consider how long you plan to keep the car and what range drop-off you're comfortable with over time.

Charging your Leaf in NZ

🏠
Home charging
Most Leaf owners charge overnight using a dedicated wall unit or appropriate socket. We can talk through the setup you'll likely need and what to check with your electrician before the car arrives.
⚡
Public charging
NZ's public network continues to grow, with fast chargers on main routes and slower options in towns and shopping areas. Once we know your Leaf's hardware, we'll explain how it works with NZ providers.
🔌
Cables & hardware
The charging cables and portable units in Japanese Leafs may not suit NZ sockets directly. We'll flag what you'll need to source locally before or after the car arrives.
Worth knowing
  • Charging speeds depend on your specific Leaf's hardware and the charger you plug into.
  • Some Japanese portable chargers may need replacing for safe NZ use.
  • Heavy reliance on public fast charging can affect long-term battery health — we'll talk through this.

Common questions

What range can I expect from a 2026 Leaf in NZ?
Depends on battery size and how you drive. Current Leafs with 40–62 kWh batteries offer WLTP ranges of roughly 270–385 km. We'll match you with a battery option that suits your use.
How much does it cost to import one from Japan?
Total cost varies with auction price, battery spec, mileage and grade. On top of the auction price: shipping, insurance, port charges, entry certification, compliance work and our fee.
Will a 2026 Leaf meet NZ entry standards?
Based on auction documentation, this model typically meets NZ entry standards if docs are clean and there are no non-compliant modifications. Final approval is confirmed after physical inspection in NZ.
How long does shipping to NZ take?
Once we win an auction, shipping typically takes around 2–4 weeks depending on the sailing schedule and port. Allow extra time for booking, processing, certification and registration.
How do auction grades relate to actual condition?
Grades are a shorthand — the comments and diagrams matter just as much. We send you the full sheet, photos and our plain-language explanation so you can judge whether that car suits you.
