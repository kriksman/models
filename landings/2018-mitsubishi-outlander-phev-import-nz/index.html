Battery health

Checking Outlander PHEV battery health from Japan

On a 2018 Outlander PHEV, the 12 kWh battery’s condition has a direct impact on how much EV-only driving you’ll get and how low your running costs can be. While we can’t test the battery ourselves until the car is in NZ, we use the available Japanese documentation to reduce guesswork before bidding.

Look for recent battery SOH readings or dealer inspection notes where available.
Avoid auction sheets that mention hybrid system faults, repeated warning lights or no-start conditions.
Prefer cars with regular servicing at Mitsubishi dealers, especially if hybrid system checks are noted.
Consider mileage and usage pattern — high kilometres with little mention of charging may mean more wear on the battery.
Allow some budget for future battery or hybrid system maintenance as the car ages.

Home charging

Charging a 2018 Outlander PHEV in NZ

Most owners charge at home using a standard NZ power outlet or a dedicated wall unit. Overnight charging is usually enough to refill the 12 kWh battery, so your first 20–35 km of each day can often be electric if you plug in regularly.

Public charging

The Outlander PHEV uses a CHAdeMO fast charging port, which is supported at many public DC chargers around NZ, though CCS is becoming more common. You can still rely on AC charging at home and use CHAdeMO when it’s convenient rather than as your only option.

  • • Public charging costs and availability vary by provider and region, so it’s worth checking local networks you’ll actually use.
  • • CHAdeMO is well established but newer sites may prioritise CCS, so long-term you may lean more on home charging.
  • • Fast charging too often can add extra heat cycles to the battery; regular home charging is usually gentler for day-to-day use.

FAQ

Questions before importing

What real-world fuel use can I expect from a 2018 Outlander PHEV in NZ?

Most owners see around 4–5L/100km if they plug in regularly and do a mix of city and open-road driving. If you rarely charge or mostly do long highway trips, fuel use will be higher and closer to a conventional SUV.

How much does it cost to import a 2018 Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV from Japan?

Most 2018 Outlander PHEVs we source land between about $22,000 and $35,000, depending on mileage, grade and features. Your total cost includes the auction price, our fee, shipping and insurance, NZ entry certification, and on-road costs like registration and plates.

Will a 2018 Outlander PHEV meet NZ compliance standards?

Based on auction documentation, this model usually meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval depends on physical inspection in NZ, so we avoid cars with obvious red flags in the paperwork.

How long does it take from winning an auction to driving the car in NZ?

From a successful bid, shipping typically takes 2–4 weeks depending on the sailing schedule, then you need time for NZ entry certification and registration. In most cases you’re looking at roughly 6–10 weeks all up, but port congestion and workshop queues can move that around.

What auction grade should I look for on an Outlander PHEV?

Grades 4 and above with a tidy interior grade are usually where the better cars sit, but the comments and photos matter as much as the number. We go through the sheet with you, point out any notes on the hybrid system, body repairs or corrosion, and explain why a car looks reasonably well bought or not.
