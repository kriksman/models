What a Japan-import Leaf gets you

The Nissan Leaf has been one of the most common EVs in Japan for years, which means a wide choice of 24 kWh, 30 kWh, 40 kWh and 62 kWh battery packs at auction. Earlier 24 kWh and 30 kWh cars are typically best for shorter city use, while 40 kWh models give many NZ drivers around 200–240 km of real-world range and the 62 kWh Leaf e+ can reach around 320–340 km depending on driving style and conditions. Motor output ranges from about 80 kW in early cars up to around 160 kW in the Leaf e+, so performance steps up as you move through generations. With the right battery health and charging setup, a Leaf can cut most households’ transport energy costs compared with a similar-sized non-EV hatch.

Battery health on imported Nissan Leafs

Battery condition is the key factor in how useful a used Nissan Leaf will be for you. We look at state of health data where available, age, mileage and how the car appears to have been used to build a picture of likely remaining range. While we can’t test the battery ourselves until the car reaches NZ, we use the best information from Japan to focus on cars that look like a good fit from the start.

✓

Target higher SOH percentages where possible, especially for smaller 24 kWh and 30 kWh packs.

✓

✓

✓

✓

Charging07

Charging a Nissan Leaf in NZ

At home

Most Nissan Leaf owners in NZ charge at home overnight using a dedicated wall unit on a suitable circuit. This lets you start most days with a full battery and makes smaller 24 kWh and 30 kWh packs very workable for city use.

Public network

For longer trips or if you can’t always charge at home, public fast chargers can add useful range in a short stop. Many Leafs from Japan use CHAdeMO fast charging, so it’s worth checking local charger availability on your usual routes.

!

Older Leafs may charge more slowly at fast chargers if the battery is warm or has aged.

!

Some Japanese domestic charging cables aren’t suitable for NZ sockets, so you may need a local solution.

!

If you rely heavily on public charging, factor those costs and any wait times into your decision.

FAQ16

Questions before importing

What real-world range can I expect from a Nissan Leaf in NZ?
As a guide, many drivers see roughly 100–130 km from a 24 kWh Leaf, 130–160 km from 30 kWh, around 200–240 km from 40 kWh and about 320–340 km from a 62 kWh Leaf e+, depending on driving style, terrain and battery health.
How much does it cost to import a Nissan Leaf from Japan?
Total cost depends on battery size, year, condition and auction price on the day. Your landed cost is the Japan auction price plus shipping, insurance, NZ port fees, entry certification, compliance, our fee and on-road costs like registration and plates.
Will my imported Nissan Leaf meet NZ entry standards?
We target Leafs that typically meet NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval is decided at NZ entry certification after physical inspection, so we can’t promise an outcome in advance.
How long does it take to get a Nissan Leaf from Japan to NZ?
Once we win an auction, shipping to NZ usually takes around 2–4 weeks depending on the sailing schedule. Allow extra time for booking, port handling, entry certification and compliance before you can drive the car.
What auction grade should I look for on a Nissan Leaf?
Many buyers aim for grade 4 or better with a tidy interior grade, but a well-documented grade 3.5 can also be worth considering. We explain the grade, translate the notes and share photos so you can weigh condition against price.
