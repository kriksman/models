Built for NZ driving

Why Kiwis buy this import

The 2022 Nissan Leaf is a practical way to go electric for commuting, school runs and weekend trips without using a non-EV car at all. The 40 kWh version typically gives around 220–240 km of mixed NZ driving, while the 62 kWh Leaf e+ can usually manage 320–340 km if the battery is in good shape. Later-model Leafs add stronger safety tech and more range than early cars, so for many households this replaces a main car, not just a city runabout.

Battery health

Battery health on a 2022 Nissan Leaf

For a 2022 Leaf, the battery is still relatively new, but there can already be differences in State of Health depending on how the car was used and charged in Japan. On a good example, you’d expect SOH to still be in the high 80s to mid 90s percent range, which helps the 40 kWh cars stay near 220–240 km and the 62 kWh Leaf e+ closer to 320–340 km in mixed NZ use. We look for consistent SOH readings, moderate mileage and no signs of heavy rapid-charging use, then share that information with you before you decide to bid. Once the car lands in NZ, you can choose to have an independent battery scan done for extra peace of mind.

Home charging

Charging a 2022 Leaf in NZ

Most 2022 Leaf owners in NZ charge at home overnight using a dedicated wall unit, which is kinder to the wiring and usually faster than a standard socket. You can set charging times to line up with cheaper night rates if your power company offers them.

Public charging

On the road, the Leaf can use public AC chargers for top-ups and DC fast chargers for quicker stops on longer trips. How often you’ll need public charging depends on your daily distance and whether you choose the 40 kWh or 62 kWh battery.

  • • If you live in an apartment or have no off-street parking, think through how and where you’ll charge before you commit.
  • • Fast-charging is handy but relying on it heavily can affect long-term battery health, so it’s best as an occasional tool rather than your only charging method.
  • • Public charger availability can vary by region, so check your local network coverage if you often drive outside main centres.
