Battery health

Battery health: the key factor for any Leaf E+ G

On a used Leaf E+ G, battery condition matters more than almost anything else. We look beyond the odometer to how the car has been used and what the auction notes say about the high-voltage system. That helps you choose a car with range and longevity that match how you plan to drive it.

Look for comments about reduced range, warning lights or EV system faults on the auction sheet.
Favour cars that appear to have been privately owned rather than heavy fleet or taxi use.
Check for signs of frequent rapid charging, which can affect long-term capacity.
Plan for some natural battery degradation over time and match the car to your daily distance.

Home charging

Most Leaf E+ G owners in NZ charge at home overnight using a dedicated wall unit or upgraded socket. We can point you towards local installers so you can plan home charging before the car arrives.

FAQ

Questions before importing

What real-world range can I expect from a Nissan Leaf E+ G in NZ?

Range depends on battery health, driving style and terrain. A healthy E+ G battery typically gives noticeably more range than earlier Leafs, enough for most commutes and regular weekend trips. We’ll talk through realistic figures based on the specific car’s condition.

How much does it cost to import a Leaf E+ G to NZ?

Total cost includes the auction price in Japan, our service fee, shipping and insurance, NZ entry certification, and on-road costs. Once we know the sort of car you’re targeting, we can give you a working landed estimate so you can compare it with NZ dealer pricing.

Will a Leaf E+ G usually meet NZ entry standards?

Based on auction documentation, this model usually meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval depends on physical inspection at NZ entry certification, which no one can guarantee in advance.

How long does it take from winning an auction to driving the car in NZ?

From a successful bid, shipping to NZ is typically 2–4 weeks depending on the sailing schedule, then allow time for port handling, entry certification and registration. usually you’re looking at several weeks rather than months, but timing can vary with shipping and workshop queues.

How do auction grades relate to the condition of a Leaf E+ G?

Auction grades give a quick view of exterior, interior and overall condition, but the comments and diagrams matter just as much. We go through these with you, highlight any repairs or wear, and share all photos so you’re not relying on the grade alone.
