What sets it apart03

The clever bits

On a Discovery HSE, the worthwhile features are the ones that make a large SUV easier to place, load and live with.

Terrain Response

Terrain Response is one of the Discovery's key systems because it changes throttle, gearbox and traction settings to suit the surface underneath you. That matters in NZ because a big family SUV often ends up doing more than sealed-road commuting, even if it is mostly used as a daily driver.

Power tailgate

A power tailgate is more useful on a Discovery than on a smaller SUV because the opening is large and the load height matters when you are dealing with bags, sports gear or a pram. It is one of those features buyers appreciate every week rather than once a year.

Surround-view camera system

Large SUVs live or die by visibility in car parks, and a camera system makes a real difference when placing the Discovery in tighter spaces. It is also handy when checking kerbs, towball alignment or backing into a narrow driveway.

Driver-assist package

Higher-spec Discovery grades commonly include adaptive cruise and lane-support functions that take some of the effort out of longer motorway runs. On a vehicle built for covering distance, that is the sort of technology worth paying attention to.

Fitted technology04

What is actually fitted

The Discovery makes more sense when you look at the systems that shape how it drives and how easy it is to use.

01

HSE trim

HSE stands for High Specification Equipment, and in Discovery terms it is the grade many buyers target because it usually brings the cabin and convenience features that suit a premium family SUV. Rather than paying for the badge alone, you are generally stepping into the part of the range where the seats, infotainment and driver-assist equipment feel complete. That matters on an import because trim level can change the ownership experience more than the odometer does.

02

Terrain Response four-wheel-drive control

Terrain Response is Land Rover's drive management system for changing how the vehicle responds to different surfaces. It adjusts the drivetrain and traction settings to suit conditions, which helps a heavy SUV feel more composed when the road turns loose or slippery. Even if the car spends most of its life on sealed roads, the system is part of why a Discovery feels more sure-footed than a soft-roader.

03

Air suspension on equipped vehicles

Many Discovery HSE examples are fitted with air suspension, and it is one of the reasons these drive with that settled long-distance feel. It can also help with access height and load levelling, which is useful when passengers or luggage vary from trip to trip. The flip side is that it is a system worth checking carefully through auction notes, service history and later NZ inspection because complexity is part of the ownership picture.

Engine and drivetrain

The petrol Discovery HSE is bought more for refinement and smooth power delivery than for low running costs. In a vehicle this size, that matters, because a smoother petrol setup can make town driving, motorway merging and long-distance touring feel easier and quieter. The Discovery's four-wheel-drive hardware is also a big part of the appeal, giving it a more serious drivetrain layout than many crossover SUVs. If you are comparing it with softer seven-seat options, this is one of the reasons it feels like a proper large SUV rather than just a tall wagon.
