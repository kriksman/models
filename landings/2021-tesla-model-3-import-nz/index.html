Model and compliance notes

Here’s how different 2021 Tesla Model 3 variants and options tend to play out when importing from Japan.

Year range
For a 2021 Tesla Model 3 you’re well inside current NZ entry rules, including ESC and safety requirements. Later build months may have small software or trim tweaks, which we can confirm from the auction sheet and VIN.
Powertrain or battery options
Japan auctions list Standard Range and Long Range variants, with different quoted ranges and performance. Long Range suits regular intercity trips, while Standard Range can work if most driving is local and you want to keep landed cost down.
Key trim differences
Spec differences usually show up in wheel design, interior trim colour, audio options and driver‑assist packages. We highlight which features each car has based on photos and auction notes so you’re not guessing.
NZ compliance notes
A 2021 Model 3 typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. As an EV it’s subject to current CO2 rules but avoids running cost‑burn penalties. We still allow for entry certification costs and any remedial work in the landed estimate.
Auction availability
Tesla Model 3s appear regularly in major Japanese auctions, but clean, low‑km Long Range cars can attract strong bidding. If you’re flexible on colour or trim, it can shorten the wait and help with price.

Battery health

Battery condition is central to whether a 2021 Tesla Model 3 makes sense for you. While we can’t run our own diagnostics until the car is in NZ, we use auction data, mileage and service records to build a picture of how each car has been used. FAQ

Questions before importing

What real‑world range can I expect from a 2021 Tesla Model 3 in NZ?

The quoted 353 miles for the Long Range model converts to about 570 km, but real‑world NZ range is usually lower. How and where you drive, temperature and use of heating or air con all play a part, so we talk through what’s realistic for your driving pattern.

How much does it cost to import a 2021 Tesla Model 3 from Japan?

Most 2021 Model 3s we see land between about $60,000 and $80,000, depending on variant, mileage and condition. The total includes the auction price, shipping and insurance, NZ entry certification, port charges, our fee and on‑road costs like registration and plates.

Will a 2021 Tesla Model 3 from Japan meet NZ compliance standards?

A 2021 Model 3 typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted, but final sign‑off only happens at NZ entry certification. We avoid cars with obvious red flags in the auction notes to improve the odds of a smooth process.

How long does it take from winning an auction to driving the car in NZ?

Once the car is bought, shipping to NZ usually takes 2–4 weeks depending on the sailing schedule, then you need time for border checks, entry certification and registration. In practice, most buyers see 6–10 weeks from auction win to driving, with some variation.

What sort of condition can I expect from a used 2021 Tesla Model 3 at auction?

Auction grades give a good guide to exterior and interior condition, but they’re still a snapshot. We review the sheet, photos and notes, and call out things like scratches, wheel rash or interior wear so you know what you’re buying before we bid.
