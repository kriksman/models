Home charging

Charging a Mercedes-Benz EQE 450 4MATIC SUV in NZ

With a Type 2 AC connection, the EQE 4MATIC SUV can be charged at home using a wallbox or, more slowly, from a standard power point. Most owners set up overnight charging so the car starts each day with a full or near-full battery, which keeps day-to-day running simple.

Public charging

On the road, the EQE 450 4MATIC SUV uses CCS for DC fast charging, which matches the main NZ rapid charging networks. That makes it suitable for city use and inter-city trips, as long as you plan stops around the current charger map.

FAQ

Questions before importing

Can a Mercedes-Benz EQE 450 4MATIC SUV handle typical NZ driving?

It’s well suited to city commuting, motorway runs and regional trips, with dual-motor all-wheel drive for wet or hilly roads. We’ll talk through your usual routes to make sure the expected range and charging options work for you.

How much does it cost to import an EQE 450 4MATIC SUV from Japan?

Total cost depends on the auction price, mileage, year, trim and shipping at the time. On top of the auction price you’ll have shipping, insurance, NZ entry certification, compliance work, registration and our service fee. We give you a full estimate before bidding.

Will a Japanese EQE 450 4MATIC SUV meet NZ entry standards?

We target vehicles that typically meet NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval is decided at NZ entry certification, so we avoid cars with obvious red flags on the auction sheet.

How long does it take from winning an auction to driving in NZ?

From a successful bid, shipping to NZ usually takes around 2–4 weeks depending on sailings, then you need time for clearance, entry certification, any required work and registration. Overall, many buyers see 6–10 weeks, but it can vary.

What auction grade should I look for on an EQE 450 4MATIC SUV?

Most buyers aim for higher auction grades with tidy interiors and minimal exterior repairs. We explain the grade, translate key auction sheet comments and flag any notes about repairs or battery-related concerns before you decide to bid.
