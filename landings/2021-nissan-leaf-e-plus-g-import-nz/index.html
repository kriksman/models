How the 2021 Leaf E+ G compares

Here’s how the 2021 Nissan Leaf E+ G typically lines up when you’re weighing it against other Leaf variants and similar EVs.

Year range
The E+ G with the 62 kWh battery is available from 2021 onwards. For NZ, later years usually mean newer safety tech and less battery age, and all 2021 cars meet current ESC requirements for entry.
Engine options
The E+ G runs a 62 kWh battery and 160 kW motor, while standard Leafs use a smaller battery and lower-output motor. In practice, the E+ G gives more range and stronger acceleration, which helps if you do regular highway or inter-city driving.
Key trim differences
E+ G models commonly add 17-inch alloys, more equipment and the higher-capacity battery compared with base trims. Some cars have extra options like upgraded audio or additional safety packs, which we check on the auction sheet for each vehicle.
NZ compliance notes
As a 2021 EV, the Leaf E+ G typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. There’s no Clean Car fee for EVs, but rules can change, so we confirm current CO2 charges or rebates and flag any entry certification risks before you commit.
Auction availability
2021 Leaf E+ G models appear regularly at major Japanese auctions, but good grades with low mileage attract strong bidding. That means you may wait a few weeks for the right car at the right price, especially if you’re picky about colour and options.

How much does it cost to import a 2021 Nissan Leaf E+ G?
Most buyers land a 2021 Leaf E+ G between about $32,000 and $42,000, depending on mileage, grade and options. The total includes the auction price, our fee, inland transport in Japan, shipping, insurance, NZ port charges, compliance work and on-road costs.
Will my Leaf E+ G meet NZ compliance and WoF requirements?
A 2021 Leaf E+ G in standard condition typically meets NZ entry standards and can go on to get a WoF once any required work is done. We can’t promise a specific car will pass before inspection, but we use the auction sheet and photos to avoid obvious compliance risks.
How long does it take from winning an auction to driving the car in NZ?
From a successful bid, shipping to NZ usually takes around 2–4 weeks depending on the sailing schedule. Allow extra time for port processing, entry certification, any compliance repairs and registration, so overall you’re often looking at roughly 6–10 weeks.
What auction grade should I look for on a 2021 Leaf E+ G?
Many buyers aim for grades around 4 or better with a clean interior grade, but the right choice depends on budget and how fussy you are about cosmetic marks. We explain what each grade and note means, share the documentation and photos, and give our view on condition before you decide to bid.
