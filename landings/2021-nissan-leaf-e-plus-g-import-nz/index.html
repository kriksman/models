Model and compliance notes

Here’s how the 2021 Nissan Leaf E+ G typically lines up when you’re comparing options to import.

Year range
The E+ G with the 62 kWh battery appears from around 2019 onward, with 2021 cars benefiting from newer battery age and updated safety tech. Later build dates can help with future resale and may show slightly better SOH on average, but price is usually higher.
Engine options
As a pure EV there’s no engine choice, but there are two main battery sizes in the Leaf range: smaller 40 kWh packs and the 62 kWh pack in the E+ models. The 62 kWh version suits longer commutes and more open-road use, while the 40 kWh cars are more city-focused and usually cheaper.
Key trim differences
The E+ G grade typically adds the larger 62 kWh battery, 160 kW motor, 17-inch alloys and more safety and comfort features compared with lower trims. Some cars may have extra options like ProPILOT, upgraded audio or different interior trims, which we can confirm from the auction sheet.
NZ compliance notes
2021 Leafs generally in most cases meet NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. As an EV there’s no Clean Car fee, and many buyers may qualify for a rebate depending on current government settings at the time of registration.
Auction availability
The 62 kWh Leaf E+ G is common enough in Japan that there’s usually a steady flow of cars, but not as many as the 40 kWh models. That means you may wait a little longer for the right combination of mileage, SOH and price, but it also helps avoid having to compromise too much on condition.

FAQ

Questions before importing

What real-world range can I expect from a 2021 Leaf E+ G in NZ?

The official WLTP figure is up to 385 km, but real-world range in NZ is usually lower. Many owners see around 260–320 km depending on SOH, driving style, speed, terrain and use of heating or air-con.

How much does it cost to import a 2021 Leaf E+ G from Japan?

Most 2021 Leaf E+ Gs we see land between about $32,000 and $42,000. The total depends on auction price, mileage, battery SOH, shipping, insurance, port charges, NZ entry work and our fee.

Will a 2021 Leaf E+ G usually meet NZ compliance requirements?

Based on auction documentation, this model usually meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval always depends on physical inspection at NZ entry certification.

How long does it take from winning an auction to driving the car in NZ?

From a successful bid, shipping is typically 2–4 weeks depending on sailings, plus time at the NZ port and entry certification. In practice, many buyers see around 6–10 weeks in total, but it can vary with shipping schedules and workshop capacity.

How do auction grades relate to the condition of a Leaf E+ G?

Auction grades give a quick view of exterior, interior and overall condition, but the comments and photos matter just as much. We translate key notes, flag any repairs or heavy wear, and send you the full sheet so you can judge whether the condition suits your expectations.
