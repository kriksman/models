What makes the Mercedes EQE 350 AMG Line 2024 practical?

The 2024 Mercedes EQE 350 AMG Line pairs a 90.6 kWh battery with a single motor rated at 288 hp and about 530 Nm, giving you 0–100 km/h in around 6.2 seconds without using any non-EV. Model and compliance notes

Here’s how different 2024 Mercedes EQE 350 AMG Line options from Japan can vary for NZ buyers.

Year range
The EQE 350 AMG Line starts from 2024 in this shape, so you’re looking at very new cars that in most cases meet NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Being new also helps with remaining manufacturer battery and vehicle warranty.
Powertrain or battery options
Most Japanese EQE 350 AMG Line cars use the 90.6 kWh battery with a single motor and rear‑wheel drive, though some markets offer 4MATIC all‑wheel drive. RWD suits NZ city and motorway use well, while 4MATIC can appeal if you often drive in wet or hilly conditions.
Key trim differences
AMG Line brings sportier exterior styling, wheels and interior trim over standard EQE models. Some cars add options like upgraded audio, larger wheels or extra driver assistance features, which we’ll highlight in the auction notes so you can weigh value vs ride comfort and tyre costs.
NZ compliance notes
As a late‑model EV, the EQE 350 AMG Line typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. There’s no exhaust emissions testing, but EV‑specific systems still need to pass inspection. We factor any EV‑related checks into the landed cost estimate.
Auction availability
The EQE 350 AMG Line is available in Japan but not in huge numbers, so you may wait a little for the right colour, mileage and option mix. Higher‑spec AMG Line cars with low km tend to attract more bidding competition, which can nudge auction prices up.

Home charging

Charging a Mercedes EQE 350 AMG Line in NZ

At home, most owners will use an AC wall unit on a Type 2 connection. Overnight charging is usually enough to top up daily use, and for many households a weekly deep charge will cover commuting and local running around. We can talk through cable options and what to ask your electrician.

Public charging

On the road, the EQE 350 AMG Line uses CCS Combo for DC fast charging, which is supported across most major NZ charging networks. For inter‑city trips, a quick top‑up at a fast charger can add useful range while you grab a coffee, especially if you start with a near‑full battery.

  • • Fast‑charging too often can add wear to any EV battery over time, so it’s best kept for trips rather than daily use.
  • • Charging speeds can vary by charger brand, temperature and how full the battery already is.
  • • If you live in an apartment or shared parking, you’ll need to confirm what charging options are allowed before committing.

FAQ

Questions before importing

What real‑world range can I expect from a 2024 Mercedes EQE 350 AMG Line in NZ?

The official figure is up to 590 km WLTP, but real‑world range in NZ will vary with speed, temperature and terrain. On flatter routes like Hamilton or Christchurch, many drivers will see range close to the WLTP number; in hilly or windy areas you should plan on less and allow a buffer.

How much does it cost to import a 2024 EQE 350 AMG Line from Japan?

Most examples land between about $120,000 and $145,000 NZD, depending on mileage, options and auction competition. That total includes the auction price, shipping, insurance, NZ taxes, port charges and entry certification‑related costs.

Will a Japanese‑import EQE 350 AMG Line meet NZ entry standards?

A 2024 EQE 350 AMG Line is very new and, based on auction documentation, this model usually meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval depends on physical inspection in NZ, so we never promise a typically goes through compliance before it’s checked.

How long does it take from winning an auction to driving the car in NZ?

Allow roughly 6–10 weeks in total. Shipping is typically 2–4 weeks depending on sailings, with the rest taken up by export handling in Japan, port processing in NZ and entry certification. Delays can happen around shipping schedules and inspection workloads.

How do auction grades relate to the condition of a premium EV like this?

Auction grades give a quick view of exterior and interior condition, but the comments and diagrams matter just as much. We translate the key points, highlight any repairs, wheel damage or interior wear, and share all documentation so you can judge whether the condition matches the price.
