Model and compliance notes

Here’s how different EQE 350 AMG Line examples from Japan can vary and what that means in NZ.

Year range
The EQE 350 AMG Line is a new model, so you’re mainly looking at 2024-onwards cars. Being new, these typically meet the latest NZ entry standards, including ESC and safety requirements, if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted.
Powertrain or battery options
The EQE 350 AMG Line uses a 90.6-kWh battery with a rear-drive motor. Differences between cars are more about software, wheel size and equipment than the core battery, but usage patterns in Japan can still affect real-world range.
Key trim differences
AMG Line cars may vary by wheel design, interior trim, audio options and driver-assistance packages. Some will have extra comfort features or upgraded infotainment, so checking the auction sheet and photos closely matters.

As a late-model EV, the EQE 350 AMG Line typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean. You’ll need to factor in any applicable CO2 charges and standard entry certification checks, but there’s no exhaust emissions testing like a non-EV car.
Auction availability
Being a premium EV, the EQE 350 AMG Line appears less often than mass-market models. That can mean waiting a little longer for the right colour, mileage and spec, but it also helps keep quality high when we shortlist cars.

FAQ

Questions before importing

What real-world range can I expect from a 2024 EQE 350 AMG Line in NZ?

The 90.6-kWh battery has an estimated range of about 480 km, but real-world numbers depend on speed, temperature, hills and how you drive. In NZ conditions, many owners will see less than the estimate, yet it’s still plenty for daily commuting and most weekend trips with sensible charging habits.

How much does it cost to import a 2024 Mercedes EQE 350 AMG Line from Japan?

Recent examples typically land between $120,000 and $140,000, depending on mileage, condition and auction price. On top of the hammer price you’ll have shipping, insurance, NZ entry certification, our service fee and on-road costs such as registration and any CO2 charges.

Will a Japanese-import EQE 350 AMG Line meet NZ entry standards?

A 2024 EQE 350 AMG Line is a modern EV that in most cases meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval always depends on physical inspection and checks at NZ entry certification.

How long does it take from winning an auction to having the car on my driveway?

From a successful bid, shipping to NZ usually takes 2–4 weeks depending on the sailing schedule, then you need time for port processing, entry certification and registration. Overall, many buyers see their car in roughly 6–10 weeks, but rare models or shipping delays can stretch that out.

How do auction grades work for a car like the EQE 350 AMG Line?

Japanese auctions grade both the body and interior, and note any repairs or damage. We send you the full auction sheet, photos and our explanation of the grade so you can see how tidy the car is compared with typical NZ stock before deciding to bid.
