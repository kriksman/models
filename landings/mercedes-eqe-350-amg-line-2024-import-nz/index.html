Model and compliance notes

Here’s how different EQE 350 AMG Line options from Japan typically compare for NZ buyers.

Year range
The EQE 350 AMG Line starts from 2024 in this spec, so all examples meet current ESC and safety feature expectations. Being new also helps with battery warranty coverage, depending on the specific car and market origin.
Powertrain or battery options
All EQE 350 AMG Line models use a 90.6 kWh battery and a single motor rated at 288 hp, with rear‑wheel drive or 4MATIC all‑wheel drive. RWD usually offers slightly better range, while 4MATIC adds traction for wet or unsealed roads.
Key trim differences
AMG Line brings sportier exterior styling, AMG wheels and upgraded interior trim over standard EQE models. Some cars add options like premium audio, upgraded driver assistance packs or different wheel sizes that can affect ride comfort and range.
NZ compliance notes
As a late‑model EV, the EQE 350 AMG Line typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. We check for any structural repairs, lighting changes or aftermarket parts that could slow entry certification.
Auction availability
Being a newer premium EV, numbers in Japan are growing but still limited compared with mass‑market models. That can mean waiting a little longer for the right colour, mileage and option mix, but it also helps keep quality higher.

Battery health

Battery health on the EQE 350 AMG Line

The EQE’s 90.6 kWh pack is designed for long‑distance use, but how it’s been charged and driven still matters. When we shortlist cars, we look for battery data, age, mileage and any notes about charging behaviour so you’re not guessing about future range.

Confirm any available battery state‑of‑health figures and how they were measured.
Check vehicle age and mileage against typical EQE degradation patterns.
Look for comments about frequent DC fast charging or taxi/fleet use in the auction notes.
Confirm that high‑voltage system warnings or EV‑related fault codes are not mentioned.
Factor in remaining manufacturer battery warranty where applicable to the specific car.

Home charging

Charging a Mercedes EQE 350 AMG Line in NZ

With a Type 2 / CCS Combo port, the EQE 350 AMG Line is straightforward to charge at home using a wallbox or suitable socket. Most owners top up overnight, which is usually enough to cover several days of commuting given the 90.6 kWh battery and typical NZ driving patterns.

Public charging

On the road, the CCS Combo connection works with most NZ fast‑charging networks. The EQE’s range means you can often skip chargers on shorter trips, but for longer runs we’ll help you think through likely stops based on where you live and drive.

  • • Some older public chargers may have lower power output, so plan a bit more time on those stops.
  • • Apartment or shared parking setups can need extra planning and body corporate approval for home charging.
  • • Fast charging is handy, but relying on it heavily over time can affect long‑term battery health.

FAQ

Questions before importing

What real‑world range can I expect from a 2024 EQE 350 AMG Line in NZ?

With a 90.6 kWh battery and up to 590 km WLTP range, many NZ drivers see less than that in practice. In mixed city and motorway use, planning around 400–500 km between charges is a sensible starting point, depending on speed, weather and how you drive.

How much does it cost to import a 2024 EQE 350 AMG Line from Japan?

Recent examples usually land between about $120,000 and $145,000, depending on mileage, options and auction price. On top of the hammer price you’ll have our fee, shipping and insurance, NZ entry work, registration and on‑road costs.

Will a Japanese‑import EQE 350 AMG Line meet NZ entry standards?

As a new premium EV, this model typically meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final entry certification is done in NZ, so we can’t confirm the outcome until the car is inspected here.

How long does the whole import process take?

Once we win an auction, shipping to NZ is usually 2–4 weeks depending on the vessel schedule. Allow extra time to find the right car, clear it through the port and complete NZ entry certification before you can drive it home.

How do auction grades relate to the condition of an EQE 350 AMG Line?

Auction grades give a quick view of exterior, interior and overall condition, but the detail sits in the written notes and photos. We translate and explain these, point out any repairs or wear, and only recommend cars where the documentation lines up with what you’re expecting.
