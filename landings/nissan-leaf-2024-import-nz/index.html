Battery06

Battery health on a 2024 Leaf

On a 2024 Nissan Leaf, the high‑voltage battery is central to range, resale and day‑to‑day confidence. While these packs are new‑generation and should be in good shape, usage patterns and charging habits still matter. We use auction documentation and available photos to avoid cars with early signs of battery issues.

✓

Look for any mention of battery checks, replacement or warranty work in the auction notes.

✓

Check for EV system warning lights or error messages in dashboard photos.

✓

Favour cars with typical yearly mileage rather than unusually high use in a short time.

✓

Avoid listings that hint at heavy rapid‑charging use where that information is provided.

✓

Consider your own range needs so we can target the right battery size and condition.

Charging07

Charging a 2024 Nissan Leaf in NZ

At home

Most Leaf owners in NZ charge at home using a dedicated wall unit or upgraded socket, topping up overnight when power demand is lower. We can talk through what sort of home charging setup suits your driving and how long a typical charge will take.

Public network

Across NZ there’s a growing mix of AC and DC fast chargers that work well with the Leaf. For longer trips, you’ll plan stops around these chargers, using the car’s navigation or apps to find suitable points along your route.

!

Older or slower home wiring may need checking by an electrician before installing a faster charger.

!

Fast charging is handy but relying on it heavily can affect long‑term battery health.

!

Public charger availability can vary by region, so rural drivers should plan ahead more carefully.

FAQ16

Questions before importing

What range can I expect from a 2024 Nissan Leaf in NZ?
Depending on whether you go for the 40 kWh or 62 kWh battery, a 2024 Leaf typically offers around 270–385 km WLTP. Real‑world NZ range will be lower, especially at motorway speeds or in winter, so we’ll talk through your daily use before you choose a battery size.
How much does it cost to import a 2024 Nissan Leaf from Japan?
Total cost depends on the auction price, battery size, trim level and mileage. On top of the auction price you’ll have shipping, marine insurance, NZ port charges, entry certification, compliance and our fee. We provide a worked example of the landed cost before you decide to bid.
Will a 2024 Leaf meet NZ entry standards?
A 2024 Leaf is a late‑model EV and usually meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval is only confirmed after physical inspection during NZ entry certification.
How long does it take from winning an auction to driving in NZ?
Once your Leaf is bought, shipping to NZ usually takes 2–4 weeks depending on sailings. Allow extra time for port processing, entry certification, compliance work and registration before you can drive it.
How do auction grades relate to the condition of a Leaf?
Auction grades give a shorthand view of overall condition, while the sheet and photos show details like scratches, interior wear and any EV‑related notes. We walk you through the grade, translate key comments and share our view before you decide to proceed.
