Battery health on a 2024 Nissan Leaf

On a 2024 Leaf, the 39 kWh and 62 kWh batteries should still be early in their life, but it’s worth checking every car carefully. We use auction sheets, photos and any available SOH data to avoid surprises and talk through what that means for your expected WLTP‑style range in NZ conditions.

✓

Look for SOH (state of health) readings or dash photos where available, especially on higher‑mileage cars.

✓

Check auction comments for notes about rapid charging habits, battery warnings or EV system alerts.

✓

Favour cars with consistent service history and no mention of EV system repairs unless clearly resolved.

✓

Consider your daily distance and choose 39 kWh or 62 kWh with a buffer, not right on the limit.

✓

Ask us to explain how the documented battery health is likely to feel in your typical NZ driving.

Charging07

Charging a 2024 Nissan Leaf in NZ

At home

Most 2024 Leaf owners in NZ charge at home overnight using a dedicated wall unit, topping up the 39 kWh or 62 kWh battery while power demand is lower. We can talk through typical overnight charge times and what sort of home setup makes sense for your place.

Public network

For longer trips, you can use public fast chargers to add useful range in a short stop, especially with the 62 kWh Leaf e+. We’ll help you think about how often you’re likely to use fast charging based on your routes and where chargers are on your usual paths.

!

Charging speeds and connector types can vary by market, so we confirm what the specific car supports from the auction details.

!

Frequent fast charging can affect long‑term battery health, so it’s best treated as a top‑up tool rather than your only charging method.

!

If you live in an apartment or can’t install a home charger, we’ll discuss whether public charging access near you is practical for day‑to‑day use.

Questions before importing

What WLTP range can I expect from a 2024 Nissan Leaf in NZ?
A 2024 Leaf with the 39 kWh battery is around 270 km WLTP, while the 62 kWh Leaf e+ is around 385 km WLTP. Real‑world range in NZ will vary with speed, terrain, weather and how you drive.
How much does it cost to import a 2024 Nissan Leaf from Japan?
Total cost depends on battery size, trim, mileage and auction price on the day. We start with the Japan auction price, then add shipping, insurance, NZ port charges, entry certification, compliance work and our fee to give you a realistic landed range before you commit.
Will a 2024 Nissan Leaf meet NZ entry standards?
A 2024 Leaf is a late‑model EV and usually meets NZ entry standards if documentation is clean and no modifications are noted. Final approval is only confirmed after physical inspection and entry certification in NZ.
How long does it take for a 2024 Leaf to arrive in NZ?
Once we win an auction, shipping is typically 2–4 weeks depending on the sailing schedule. Allow extra time for booking space on a vessel, port processing and NZ entry certification before you can drive it.
What auction grade should I look for on a 2024 Leaf?
Higher grades usually mean tidier condition, but the auction sheet comments and photos matter just as much. We explain the grade, point out any notes about damage or EV‑related issues and share the documentation so you can decide what you’re comfortable with.
